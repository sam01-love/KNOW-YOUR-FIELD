The Three Pillars

What success looks like
in every career

Explore each of the three requirement pillars in depth. Understanding all three is what sets truly exceptional students apart.

Academic Requirements

These are the formal educational benchmarks every student must meet. This covers subjects, grades, JAMB scores, and university admission criteria across career fields.

📖
Core WAEC/NECO Subjects
Most fields require a minimum of five credit passes at O'Level. English Language and Mathematics are compulsory for almost every career. Your remaining three subjects depend on your chosen field — sciences, arts, or commercial subjects.
O'Level
🎯
JAMB / UTME Requirements
The Joint Admissions and Matriculation Board score determines university placement. Science fields typically need 240–280+, while Social Science and Arts fields range from 180–220+. Your UTME subject combination must match your intended course.
JAMB
🏛️
Post-UTME / Direct Entry
Many universities conduct their own screening exams after JAMB. Top institutions may require 60%+ in Post-UTME. Direct Entry applies to students with OND, HND, or A-Level qualifications seeking admission into 200-Level.
University
📊
Grade Benchmarks by Level
Even before WAEC, your performance in JSS 3 assessments, BECE, and your SS1–SS2 continuous assessments build the foundation. Start targeting A's and B's in your key subjects from SS1 — don't wait until SS3.
All Levels
➗
Further Mathematics
For Engineering, Computer Science, Medicine, and Economics, Further Mathematics is not compulsory — but it is a significant competitive advantage. It demonstrates high analytical ability and opens advanced mathematical pathways.
Recommended
🌍
Studying Abroad Pathways
International universities look at your WASSCE/NECO results plus SAT, IELTS, or TOEFL scores. A strong O'Level record with 7+ A grades positions you competitively for scholarships in the UK, US, Canada, and beyond.
International

Skill Requirements

Skills are the practical abilities that turn academic knowledge into real-world performance. Start building these alongside your studies — they make all the difference.

🧪

Science & Technical Skills

For STEM and Healthcare fields

  • Laboratory safety, procedures, and accurate data recording
  • Scientific method: hypothesis, experiment, conclusion
  • Use of measuring instruments and analytical tools
  • Basic coding and computational thinking (Python, Scratch)
  • Engineering drawing and technical schematics
  • Environmental observation and field data collection
🗣️

Communication Skills

Essential for every single career

  • Clear, structured essay and report writing
  • Confident public speaking and oral presentations
  • Active listening and responding under pressure
  • Professional email and formal writing etiquette
  • Debate, argumentation, and persuasive communication
  • Reading comprehension of complex, technical material
💡

Critical Thinking & Research

For analytical and professional fields

  • Identifying problems and proposing structured solutions
  • Evaluating sources, evidence, and arguments
  • Conducting library and internet-based research
  • Data interpretation using charts, graphs, and tables
  • Logical deduction and avoidance of bias
  • Summarising and presenting complex findings clearly
💻

Digital & Technology Skills

For the modern workplace in any field

  • Microsoft Office Suite — Word, Excel, PowerPoint
  • Google Workspace — Docs, Sheets, Slides
  • Basic internet research and information literacy
  • Social media literacy and professional online presence
  • Introduction to design tools (Canva, Figma basics)
  • Data entry, spreadsheet functions, and visualisation
🎨

Creative & Artistic Skills

For creative and communication careers

  • Freehand drawing, sketching, and visual composition
  • Colour theory, typography, and visual storytelling
  • Photography fundamentals and image editing
  • Video production, storyboarding, and editing basics
  • Digital art and graphic design tools (Adobe suite)
  • Creative writing, poetry, and narrative structure
🤝

Interpersonal & Leadership Skills

For all management and people-facing roles

  • Teamwork, cooperation, and shared goal-setting
  • Conflict resolution and de-escalation techniques
  • Time management and personal organisation
  • Mentoring peers and leading group projects
  • Networking and professional relationship-building
  • Giving and receiving constructive feedback gracefully

Character Requirements

Character is what carries you through when knowledge alone isn't enough. These are the human qualities that employers, institutions, and communities look for in every professional.

💎
Integrity
Doing the right thing even when no one is watching. Professionals are trusted with information, resources, and lives — honesty is non-negotiable in every field.
🔥
Resilience & Grit
The ability to keep going after failure. Every career involves setbacks, rejections, and hard seasons. The students who succeed are those who get back up.
❤️
Empathy
Understanding and genuinely caring about other people's experiences. From medicine to law to education — empathy makes you more effective and more human in your work.
📐
Discipline & Consistency
Showing up, doing the work, and maintaining standards even when motivation fades. Great results are built through consistent small actions over time.
🌱
Curiosity & Growth Mindset
A genuine hunger to learn more, question assumptions, and grow beyond your current limits. The best professionals never stop being students of their craft.
🤲
Accountability
Taking ownership of your actions, mistakes, and results — and fixing them. Leaders and trusted professionals hold themselves accountable before others do.
🧭
Ethical Judgment
Knowing right from wrong in complex situations and having the courage to act accordingly. Many careers involve difficult ethical decisions — develop your moral compass early.
⏳
Patience
Success in any meaningful career takes years. Patience allows you to stay the course, invest in deep learning, and build things that last rather than chasing quick results.
"Character is the foundation upon which competence is built. Without it, even the most gifted student will struggle to earn the trust that a long and impactful career demands."
— Know Your Field, Student Career Guidance
